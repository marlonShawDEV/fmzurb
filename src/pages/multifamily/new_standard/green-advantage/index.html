Is Going Green Worth It?

Going green isn’t just for new construction and shiny class-A buildings. From coast to coast, property owners are rehabbing existing rental housing and making “green” upgrades a priority. Here are three big reasons why:

Green Renters

Renters are more concerned about increases in utility bills than rent or other essentials, according to a survey of renters conducted by Freddie Mac Multifamily. Nearly eight out of 10 renters said they prefer to rent a green home, and nearly half are willing to pay more rent for it.

Simple Fixes = Big Returns

Simple, practical green fixes can add up to big returns. Take this example:

At a cost of only $500 per unit, the owner of 430-unit 1990’s Texas apartment got a huge return—shown in the box below—with simple fixes like water-saving showerheads, toilets and faucet aerators.
